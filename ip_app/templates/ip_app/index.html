Understanding Your IP Address

Learn what an IP address is, why it matters, and how to protect your digital identity

An Internet Protocol (IP) address is your digital identity on the internet a unique numerical label assigned to every device connected to a network. Think of it as your home's street address, but for the online world. At WhatIsMyIPAddress.World, we help you discover what is your IP address and understand what it reveals about you.

The Two Types of IP Addresses You Should Know

IPv4 Addresses

The original and most common IP address format, consisting of four sets of numbers separated by periods (e.g., 192.168.1.1). With approximately 4.3 billion possible addresses, IPv4 addresses are becoming increasingly scarce in today's connected world.

Example: 8.8.8.8 (Google's public DNS)

IPv6 Addresses

The newer, more advanced address format using hexadecimal digits separated by colons. IPv6 was developed to solve address exhaustion and provides an almost unlimited pool of unique addresses for the growing number of internet-connected devices.

Example: 2001:4860:4860::8888 (Google's IPv6 DNS)

Understanding IP Addresses: Common Questions

Can someone hack me with just my IP address?

Simply knowing your IP address isn't enough for someone to hack your device. However, it can be used as a starting point for potential attacks. Your IP address by itself doesn't give anyone access to your personal data or device.

That said, it's still wise to protect your IP using the methods discussed above, and regularly check "what is my IP" on WhatIsMyIPAddress.World to monitor your digital footprint.

Why does my IP address keep changing?

Most residential internet connections use dynamic IP addresses that change periodically. Your ISP might reassign your IP address when:

  • You restart your router
  • Your ISP performs maintenance
  • Your DHCP lease expires (typically every 24 hours to several days)
  • You switch between networks (e.g., home WiFi to mobile data)

Using WhatIsMyIPAddress.World regularly lets you track these changes and ensure your connection is working properly.

Is my IP address the same on all my devices?

All devices connected to the same network (like your home WiFi) typically share the same public IP address. This is what shows up when you check "what is my IP address" on WhatIsMyIPAddress.World from any device on that network.

However, each device has its own private/local IP address within your network. And if you switch to a different network (like mobile data or a public WiFi), your public IP will change as well.

How accurate is IP geolocation?

When you use our IP locator at WhatIsMyIPAddress.World, you might wonder about the accuracy of the location information. IP geolocation is typically accurate to the city level (80-90% accuracy), but rarely to your exact address.

Factors affecting accuracy include:

  • Your ISP's infrastructure location (which might be in a nearby city)
  • Mobile connections showing the location of data centers, not your device
  • VPNs showing the server location rather than your actual location
  • Corporate networks routing traffic through central offices
  • Database update frequency of geolocation services

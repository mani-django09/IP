Types of IP Addresses: IPv4 vs IPv6 Explained

When you ask "what is my IP address" on WhatIsMyIPAddress.World, you might see either an IPv4 or IPv6 address. Understanding the difference is important for modern internet users:

Feature IPv4 IPv6
Format 32-bit (192.168.1.1) 128-bit (2001:db8::1)
Address Space ~4.3 billion addresses 340 undecillion addresses
Adoption Universal (current standard) Growing (future standard)
Security Added later Built-in IPSec

WhatIsMyIPAddress.World automatically detects whether you're using IPv4 or IPv6 and provides appropriate information for both protocols. As the internet transitions to IPv6 due to IPv4 address exhaustion, our tool ensures you always get accurate results regardless of your connection type.

IP Address Privacy & Security: What You Need to Know

Understanding the privacy implications of "what is my IP address" is crucial for maintaining your online security. When you check your IP on WhatIsMyIPAddress.World, you can see exactly what information is visible to websites and online services.

What Your IP Address Can Reveal:

  • Approximate geographic location (city/region level)
  • Your Internet Service Provider (ISP) name and details
  • Your timezone and approximate local time
  • Type of connection (residential, business, mobile)
  • Whether you're using VPN, proxy, or Tor services

What Your IP Address CANNOT Reveal:

  • Your exact home address or GPS coordinates
  • Your name, phone number, or personal identity
  • Your browsing history or website visits
  • Passwords, bank details, or private files
  • Real-time tracking of your physical location
